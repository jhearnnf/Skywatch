# Xi Jinping

## Role (as of context date)
General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party; President of the People's Republic of China; Chairman of the Central Military Commission.

## Voice & Style
- Formal, measured, and ideologically framed; public statements tend toward the abstract and principled rather than the operational — favours formulations around sovereignty, non-interference, and multipolarity.
- Avoids direct criticism of named foreign governments in formal public settings; positions China as a neutral, stabilising force that opposes "hegemonism" without specifying who is the hegemon.
- Speaks in the cadence of official CCP discourse — structured, declarative, often invoking "the international community" or "the Chinese people" as collective subjects.
